# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ Do's
1. Do keep your profiles clean and professional. Remember that future employers and internship coordinators may look you up on media.
2. Think before you post. Take least 10 seconds. If you would feel embarrassed reading it loud to a professor then don't post it.
3. Use privacy settings to control who sees your stuff. Check these settings every months to make sure they are still right for you.
4. Verify information before you share it. Sharing news or things that are not true can cause serious problems.
5. Use social media to connect with people in your field. Follow industry leaders. Join groups that are relevant to you. Have conversations with them.
6. Use different accounts for personal and professional use. This can help you stay organized.

## ❌ Don'ts
1. You should not share details like your phone number, hostel room number, home address or class schedule publicly.
2. You should not share anything when you are angry, emotional or sleep-deprived. You will probably regret sharing social media information like that.
3. Social media safety is important. You should not cyberbully or participate in online trolling even as a joke. Social media can cause harm and may have legal consequences for social media users.
4. You should not use the password across all your social media accounts. If one social media account gets compromised all of them will be compromised.
5. You should not accept friend and follow requests from people you do not know especially if their social media profiles look suspicious or were newly created.
6. You should not post photos of people without their permission particularly in situations they might not want publicized on social media.
