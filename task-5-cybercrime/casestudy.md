# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud in India

## What Is It?
UPI fraud is when bad people trick others into sending them money using payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe or Paytm. This is a problem in India because more and more people are using UPI. It handles over 10 billion transactions every month in 2024. These bad people have found ways to cheat people who trust these payment apps.
## How It Typically Happens
Here is one of the most common methods, step by step:

1. Someone wants to sell something so they post it on a website like OLX or a Facebook group where people buy and sell things.
2. A bad person sees the post. Contacts the seller pretending to be a buyer. They agree to pay the price the seller is asking without arguing about it. This should be a warning sign.
3. Instead of paying the seller the bad person sends a UPI collect request. This is like a request to take money from the sellers account.
4. The bad person tells the seller on the phone: "I have sent you the money approve the request so you can get it."
5. The seller does not really understand how UPI collect requests work so they enter their UPI PIN to accept the payment. Really money is being taken from their account.
6. The bad person disappears with the money. They move the money to different accounts so it is hard to find.

Another way this scam works is when bad people set up customer service phone numbers. The seller searches for a companys phone number on Google finds the number and calls it. Then the bad person tells them to install an app that lets them see the sellers screen or to share their UPI PIN.

## Who Is Usually Targeted?
- People who are using UPI for the time or who do not know much about technology
- College students selling items online
- Senior citizens
- Small business owners who handle many transactions daily

## Consequences
The people who get cheated lose their money. Sometimes all of their savings. They feel sad and embarrassed. They often do not want to tell the police what happened. Sometimes the amount of money that was stolen is not very much so the police do not investigate much. This makes the bad people bolder. When this kind of thing happens times people start to not trust online payment systems, which is bad for everyone who uses them.

**Source:** Based on publicly reported incidents covered by RBI advisories and news 
reports from The Hindu and NDTV (2023–2024).
