# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.** Do not reuse the password across different platforms. If you are having trouble remembering all of your passwords consider using a password manager like Bitwarden, which's free.

2. **Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere it's available** This includes your email accounts, social media accounts, UPI apps and college portals. While SMS-based 2FA is app-based 2FA, like Google Authenticator is even better.

3. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone.** No bank, no payment app, and no legitimate company will ever ask for these over phone or text.

4. **Don't approve UPI collect requests from unknown people.** You should remember that you never need to enter your PIN to receive money. If someone tells you otherwise it is likely a scam.

5. **Don't search for customer care numbers on Google or Google Maps.** Scammers often pay to have their numbers listed in these places. Instead always find support contacts from the app or the companys verified website.

6. **Be very careful with unsolicited emails, messages and links.** If something asks you to verify your account or claim a prize urgently it is certainly phishing. Always check the senders email address, not just the display name.

7. **Keep your phone and laptop software updated.** Security patches fix known vulnerabilities. Ignoring updates is like leaving your front door unlocked.

8. **Don't install random APKs or apps from links sent over WhatsApp or SMS.** Only download apps from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store and then check the reviews and permissions.

9. **Review your bank and UPI transaction history regularly.** Set up transaction alerts, either via SMS or push notification so you are aware of every debit, in real time.

10. **Know where to report cybercrime:**
    - **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
    - **Cyber Crime Helpline (24x7):** **1930**
    - You can also file an FIR at your local police station. Don't stay silent reporting helps catch these people and protect others.
